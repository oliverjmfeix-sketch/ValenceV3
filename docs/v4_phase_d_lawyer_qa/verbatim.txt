### QUESTION
What test is the build-up basket or available amount basket based on and when does the basket start growing?

### GOLD
The Cumulative Amount is based on the greatest of three tests: (1) 50% of cumulative Consolidated Net Income (which amount shall not be less than zero for any fiscal quarter), (2) Excess Cash Flow not required to be applied to prepay Term Loans or any other debt (such amount cannot be less than $0 for any fiscal year), (3) cumulative Consolidated EBITDA minus 140% of cumulative Consolidated Fixed Charges. All tests start growing from the first day of the fiscal quarter in which the Closing Date occurs.

### V4 ANSWER
The Cumulative Amount (build-up basket) is not a single-ratio test but a multi-source structure with a 'greatest of' earnings core: the basket selects the largest of (i) 50% of cumulative Consolidated Net Income, (ii) the Available Retained ECF Amount (cumulative Excess Cash Flow not required to prepay Term Loans under Section 2.10(f)), or (iii) 140% of LTM Consolidated EBITDA, and then adds to that result a starter amount equal to the greater of $130,000,000 or 100% of LTM Consolidated EBITDA, plus 100% of equity proceeds, retained asset sale proceeds, investment returns, and debt-to-equity conversion amounts [Definition of Cumulative Amount, p. 32]. The basket begins growing from the first day of the fiscal quarter in which the Closing Date occurs [Definition of Cumulative Amount, p. 32]. Capacity is reduced dollar-for-dollar by RP, RDP, and investment usage, and the same basket is shared with the RDP builder basket (subject to intercreditor/subordination restrictions) [Section 6.09(a)(A), p. 199].

### QUESTION
Are there any investment, prepayment of other debt or other baskets that can be reallocated and used to make restricted payments or dividends?

### GOLD
Yes, under 6.06(j) amount available for Restricted Debt Payment under 6.09(a) and amounts available for Investments under 6.03(y) can be reallocated to the making of Dividends. 6.09(a) includes the greater of $130,000,000 and 100% of Consolidated EBITDA. 6.09(a) also includes other more tailored baskets available for Restricted Debt Payments which may or may not be available for reallocation, including intercompany debt payments and payments in connection with a reorganization or IPO.

### V4 ANSWER
Yes, there are two key reallocation paths. First, the general RDP basket under Section 6.09(a)(I) (greater of $130M and 100% Consolidated EBITDA) is fungible with the general RP basket under Section 6.06(j) (same sizing) — both have action_scope='reallocable' and share a single capacity pool, so capacity not used for debt prepayments can be used for restricted payments/dividends [Section 6.06(j), p.196; Section 6.09(a)(I), p.200]. Second, the Cumulative Amount (builder basket, Definition of Cumulative Amount, p.32) is a shared pool reduced by RP usage, RDP usage, and investment usage alike — meaning investment returns (builder_source_investment_returns), retained asset-sale proceeds (builder_source_retained_asset_sale_proceeds), and equity proceeds (builder_source_other_equity_proceeds) all build capacity in the same pool available for dividends; conversely, investments or RDP payments drawn from the builder basket reduce what is available for restricted payments [Section 6.09(a)(A), p.199; Definition of Cumulative Amount, p.32]. ### QUESTION
Can any asset sale proceeds be used to make dividends?

### GOLD
Yes, Retained Asset Sale Proceeds build the Cumulative Amount which consists of proceeds from: Net Cash Proceeds from asset sales not subject to prepayment on account of Section 2.10(c)(iv), permitting proceeds from any Asset Sale using the unlimited basket 6.05(z) if such Asset Sale is a sale of a product line and the pro forma First Lien Net Leverage Ratio is 6.25x or less or if such test is no worse pro forma. Also includes asset sale proceeds not swept when First Lien Net Leverage Ratio is 5.75x or less (50% of proceeds) or 5.50x or less (100% of proceeds). Also includes Net Cash Proceeds from non-collateral assets, ordinary course asset sales, asset sales from non-ratio baskets, casualty events, and proceeds from collateral assets below de minimis thresholds of $20M/15% EBITDA individual and $40M/30% EBITDA annual.

### V4 ANSWER
Yes. Retained asset sale proceeds (those not required to be applied to mandatory prepayment under Section 2.10(c) due to leverage-based sweep tier exemptions or de minimis thresholds) flow into the Cumulative Amount builder basket as a dedicated additive source and, once accumulated there, may be used to make dividend payments [Definition of Cumulative Amount, p.32; builder_source_retained_asset_sale_proceeds, child_index 6]. Separately and independently, dividends may also be made using the general RP basket (greater of $130,000,000 or 100% of Consolidated EBITDA for the most recently ended Test Period) [Section 6.06(j), p.196], or on an unlimited basis if First Lien Leverage Ratio is ≤5.75x (or no worse than the prior test period) on a pro forma basis [Section 6.06(o), p.198], or by distributing assets (including proceeds thereof) of an Unrestricted Subsidiary [Section 6.06(p), p.198] — none of which require asset sale proceeds as a funding source.

### QUESTION
If the Borrower owns an asset/business division that has assets worth $200m, but EBITDA of such business is negative, can the Borrower dividend the asset/business division to shareholders if the First Lien Net Leverage Ratio is 6.0x?

### GOLD
Yes, because the Ratio RP basket 6.06(o) permits such transaction as long as the First Lien Net Leverage Ratio, even if above 5.75x, is no worse.

### V4 ANSWER
Yes, the Borrower can dividend the $200m asset/business division to shareholders at a 6.0x First Lien Net Leverage Ratio, using the ratio-based RP basket under Section 6.06(o) [p.198]. That basket permits unlimited dividends (including of business divisions) provided that, on a Pro Forma Basis, the First Lien Leverage Ratio does not exceed the GREATER OF (x) 5.75x or (y) the pre-transaction First Lien Leverage Ratio — meaning a no-worse test applies as an alternative to the 5.75x absolute threshold. Because the divested business has negative EBITDA, removing it from the consolidated group increases Consolidated EBITDA (the denominator), reducing the pro forma leverage ratio below the current 6.0x pre-transaction level — the no-worse branch of the OR condition is therefore satisfied even though 6.0x exceeds 5.75x. In addition, the general RP basket under Section 6.06(j) [p.196] provides a separate, unconditional dollar capacity of the greater of $130m or 100% of LTM Consolidated EBITDA (which, depending on overall EBITDA levels, may be insufficient to cover the full $200m value standing alone, making the ratio basket the primary pathway).
